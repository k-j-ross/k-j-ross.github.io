Week 1: January eighth to fourteenth

Plain Language Summary

In week one it was both cold and snowy. It was hard to be out in the cold and snow and have nerves in my legs that do not work well. I had to dress well. It was not very slippery, however walking was hard an tiring. The main theme I address in the reflective writing from the week is the extra emotional load of worrying about my body in the cold weather. My feet have a hard time feeling the cold.

Full text for week one

It is cold. Cold and snowy. I find myself afraid for my damaged and growing nerves. I am not afraid only that my legs lack stability but also afraid for the risk of damage to their ongoing sensation of the world. I miss the fine-tuned awareness of just how cold my feet and legs are. I have spent so many early mornings in winter walking or biking as my body becomes increasingly numb. I have spent so many mornings cursing internally while smiling outwardly over the increasing coldness of my appendages. This winter, I grieve that I do not feel the gradual numbing of my limbs.

Cane, step, step. I feel a weary. Every step calculated. Every choice navigated. Every slightly irritated bus driver placated. Every indication of cold in my limbs carefully calibrated.

We are corporal beings in relationship to landscape— inhabiting this corporeality is a deeply local exercise.

Cane, step, step. I am myself becoming with this land. The tiny landscape that is available to me is dominated by human construction: a human construction that is not made for my body as it is today.

So, on Friday, in the comfort of my home—thinking about how the frigid air protects my dear pine tree from pine beetles— I stare out my window, watching the world and observing. Lines of ice on glass can be a complete story of the world and a reflection of our position in it; ephemeral and constant; constrained and abundant; personal and universal.
