Week 2: January fifteenth to twenty-first

Plain Language Summary

The biggest thing I noticed this week was how the people around me make it possible to live with a disability. My family of course, but also neighbours as well. When I am in my own neighbourhood, there is more access to places than I have once I get onto the bus. I do not know if this is the same for other people. The other thing that I noticed was that there are many people who are worried about water, dryness, and drought. Some people talk to me more with a cane, others talk to me less. But many people I do talk to are worried about there not being enough snow.

Full text for week two

My experience this week can be summarised in a journey to a bus stop. A story of social infrastructure, troubled but also beautiful.

The air is still bitter, the snow is fresh. I head out, round the corner and head down the street; we waited a long time for winter. The dryness of 2023 still weighs on me. I am slowly making it to my bus stop, there is a row of people out shoveling. They see me coming. A thin line of cleared sidewalk emerges, a path through to the bottom of the hill. I pass one of my neighbours, nod my head, and say thanks and have a good day. We exchange pleasantries noting drought and precipitation.

As the snow falls it feels like there is a multitudinal sigh of relief: moisture!

I am left waiting for my bus and asking what is my responsibility in this?
