Introducroty essay

Experiences of mobility and disability are rooted in place, time, and positionality. Local infrastructures, social, physical, and emotional make us variously disabled, they make us variously mobile.

My (im)mobility is only one of these experiences. It is mediated by how landscape interacts with water. I do not use a car. I did not before I became physically disabled, and I do not now. So, as a researcher of data visualization and active mobility, I am curious about disability, snow and ice and the context of micromobility in the global north. I am curious about micro-level phenomenon: the water on my sidewalk, the rivulets at the curbside.

While (dis)ability and (im)mobility are global realities, we still live in a world where only some people who have experienced disabling infrastructures are regularly heard. Global north voices. Voices like mine.

Use my story as presented here to consider disabled encounters with water that involve trying to escape war, trying to escape persecution, trying to get to safety. Ponder living in contexts where disabled care and dignity are viewed as essential for collective thriving. Reflect on the assumptions you make about the experiences of all disabled bodies and particularly those of the global majority.

Do you assume a singular story?

This is one journey; it does not reflect the multitudes of daily water mediated disabled journeys.

My experience with disability is not universal, there is no normative body, so my experience is not representative.
