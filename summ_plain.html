Curbside | The different ways to explore

One method for building more accessible things is using many ways of communicating when we build them. I make no promises that what I have provided will be excellent in terms of accessibility, like everyone, I have much to learn. I have tried to provide a range of tools for accessing this work. Below are some points that can help you navigate this rudimentary site based on your access needs and interests.

  • The yellow rimmed QR codes will take you to a simple webpage. Here you can access alt text for the timeline and the photos, a plane text summary of my reflections and the full written text that I initially produced for the week. These are on the bottom left-hand side of each page with a printed summary. They stand out from the page and are encircled in yellow yarn.
  • Folks who engage more readily with audio then reading, The QR code in the center bottom of the description pages that are circled in red/orange provide access to audio readings of some of the complete text of the weekly reflections.
  • For those who are interested in deeper dives and thinking about things from a research perspective I provide links to selected references that I have engaged with and used, and in some cases short one to two sentences summaries. These are encircled in the blue blueberry treated yarn on the right-hand side of each weekly text.

There is not right way to engage with this work. I hope that using these different modalities and options you can find one that works for you.
