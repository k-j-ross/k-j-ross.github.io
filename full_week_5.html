Full text for week five

How can I tell you about the beauty I have noticed this week because of my slow wanderings to and from the bus? How can I write about how slow wandering tunes me into my breath, my heart, my life, reasserting bodily awareness? This slowness that moves me into and through that which is difficult. How can I tell you of these things without you walking away thinking that it makes the other stuff ok?

You will choose.

To move slowly is as valid a way of being in the world as any other, it is one I enjoy. It is also radical in a world that values busy, in a world that wants rush, in a world that pretends I can be a superhero.

Slowness this week meant noticing the very granules of snow that cling to last yearâ€™s lilac seeds. Slow means noticing how the visual metaphor of a glacier can be found in a 1-foot square.

My pace of living is out of sync with the world; how much I wish I could gently hold a moment with each frazzled soul that passes. I would critique busy culture, hustle culture, aspirational culture, but this would be unfair. It is a black hole whose gravitational pull is impossible to avoid. I too will be pulled into its gravitational well again someday. So, critique of the rush is fruitless, instead I grieve. Speed is an abled privilege. The hustle keeps me out of full participation.

When did we replace living with doing?
